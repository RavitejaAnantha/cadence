What was decided and why. (Episodic memory: a time-stamped record that the outer loop later
distills into semantic memory.)

- Decision: keep the pipeline fully offline and deterministic, with no model calls. Why: it makes the pipeline bit-reproducible, runs in CI without credentials, and keeps the eval honest.
- Decision: bake confounders (popularity exposure, position bias) into the synthetic logs. Why: it makes the offline-vs-causal point in ATTRIBUTION.md concrete rather than asserted.
- Decision: cluster the bootstrap by user, not by row. Why: eval points from one user are correlated, and a row-level bootstrap reports falsely tight CIs.
- Observation: personalized beats baseline on NDCG@5 vs true relevance, 0.432 [0.364, 0.497], CI excludes zero. The confounded click-recall metric compresses that gap, which is the point.
- Observation: baseline coverage is 0.125 (the same five popular tracks for everyone). Personalized coverage is 0.900 but with lower intra-list diversity. A real trade-off worth discussing.
- Lesson promoted to semantic memory: popularity must never enter true relevance; it is an exposure confounder only.
